## Design Context

### Users
Obsessive football fans aged 18-35. Competitive, WhatsApp-native, mobile-first. They use the app to prove they know more about football than their friends. The context is quick, intense quiz sessions — solo grinding ELO, dueling friends, or surviving battle royale elimination rounds.

### Brand Personality
**Competitive. Premium. Visceral.**

The app should feel like stepping onto a floodlit pitch — high-stakes, electric, prestigious. Not a casual pub quiz. Not a kids' trivia game. This is where football knowledge becomes a sport.

Emotional goals: intensity, competitiveness, prestige, adrenaline during gameplay, satisfaction on correct answers, hunger to improve.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Apple Liquid Glass + Modern UI**

- **Primary aesthetic:** Apple's Liquid Glass design language — translucent layered surfaces with depth, blur, and light refraction. Glass that feels alive, not flat frosted panels.
- **Dark mode primary.** Light mode only for local 2-player sessions.
- **Single accent:** iOS Blue (#007AFF) as the ONE brand color. Gold (#e6a800) for Pro/premium moments only.
- **No decorative elements:** No blobs, particles, orbs, gradients-for-decoration. Atmospheric football imagery only.
- **Typography:** Space Grotesk for display/headlines, Inter for body/UI, Lexend for data/micro-labels, Alfa Slab One for brand moments.
- **Motion:** Purposeful, fast, game-feel. Tap feedback <150ms, reveals <200ms, transitions <250ms. Spring curves for bottom sheets.
- **Anti-slop:** No opaque borders (use tonal steps), no standard drop shadows (use ambient occlusion), no dividers (use spacing), no border-radius >12px on cards.

**Color system note:** The current token palette should evolve toward a more modern, Apple-influenced feel — cooler surface tones, more sophisticated neutral hierarchy, and glass effects that rely on blur + transparency rather than flat rgba backgrounds.

### Design Principles

1. **Glass is the medium** — Every elevated surface should feel like real glass: translucent, refractive, alive with depth. Use backdrop-filter blur, subtle light borders, and layered transparency. Never flat panels with rgba backgrounds pretending to be glass.

2. **Competitiveness drives every pixel** — The UI should make users feel like athletes, not quiz show contestants. Scores, streaks, ELO ratings, and rankings should be prominent, visceral, and satisfying to watch change.

3. **Speed is a feature** — Interactions must feel instant. No loading spinners longer than 300ms without skeleton states. Tap-to-reveal under 200ms. The app should feel faster than the user's reflexes.

4. **One accent, zero noise** — iOS Blue is the only accent. Everything else is neutral glass surfaces and atmospheric imagery. Visual hierarchy comes from blur depth and surface layering, not color variety.

5. **Mobile-native, not web-adapted** — Every interaction pattern should feel like it belongs on iOS/Android. Native gesture patterns, safe area handling, haptic-ready timing. Touch targets minimum 44px. No hover states in primary flows.
